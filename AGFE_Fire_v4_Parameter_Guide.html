📊 Overview — Where Does the Data Come From?

All data originates from Google Earth Engine (GEE), a cloud-based geospatial analysis platform. We extracted pixel-level environmental, fire, terrain, and meteorological data for the Western Ghats biodiversity hotspot in India across 4 temporal blocks spanning 22 years. Each sample is a geographic pixel (~500m resolution), not an administrative area.

What is a "parameter" here? Each parameter is a pixel-level measurement extracted from satellite imagery or derived datasets at a geographic coordinate (latitude, longitude) in the Western Ghats. These are NOT area-level statistics — they are precise remote sensing values at each 500m pixel location. The data is indexed spatially (lat/lon) and temporally (block period).

2 NDVI_max 🌿 Vegetation MODIS MOD13A2 Maximum NDVI during the block period. Peak vegetation greenness — higher values = denser canopy at peak growth. Index [-1, 1] Per-pixel (500m)

5 EVI_mean 🌿 Vegetation MODIS MOD13A2 Mean Enhanced Vegetation Index. Similar to NDVI but corrects for atmospheric conditions and soil background. Better for dense canopy. Index [-1, 1] Per-pixel (500m)
6 LAI_mean 🌿 Vegetation MODIS MOD15A2H (500m, 8-day) Mean Leaf Area Index — total leaf surface area per unit ground area. Higher LAI = denser canopy, more organic fuel. m²/m² Per-pixel (500m)

9 LFMC_proxy 💧 Fuel Moisture Computed: EVI_mean / (VPD_kPa + 0.01) Live Fuel Moisture Content proxy. Low LFMC = dry, flammable vegetation. Computed from vegetation greenness divided by atmospheric dryness. Ratio Per-pixel (500m)
10 temp_mean_C 🌡️ Weather ERA5-Land (0.1°, hourly → block avg) Mean air temperature at 2m above ground, averaged over the block period. Higher temps dry out fuel and increase fire risk. °C Per-pixel (~11km, downscaled)
11 temp_max_C 🌡️ Weather ERA5-Land Maximum temperature recorded during block period. Extreme heat events drive fire ignition. °C Per-pixel (~11km)
12 temp_min_C 🌡️ Weather ERA5-Land Minimum temperature. Low night temps with high day temps = large diurnal range = faster fuel drying. °C Per-pixel (~11km)
13 precip_total_mm 🌧️ Precipitation CHIRPS (0.05°, daily → block sum) Total accumulated precipitation over block period. Low precip = drought conditions = high fire vulnerability. FINAL MODEL mm Per-pixel (~5.5km)
14 precip_mean_mm 🌧️ Precipitation CHIRPS Mean daily precipitation. Strongly correlated with precip_total_mm (ρ=1.0), hence removed by correlation filter. mm/day Per-pixel (~5.5km)
15 relative_humidity_pct 🌧️ Moisture ERA5-Land Mean relative humidity at 2m. Low humidity = fast evaporation = dry fuel = higher fire spread rate. % Per-pixel (~11km)
16 VPD_kPa 🌧️ Moisture ERA5-Land (computed) Vapor Pressure Deficit — the "drying power" of the atmosphere. High VPD = atmosphere pulls moisture from vegetation = wildfire risk. kPa Per-pixel (~11km)
17 dry_months_count 🌧️ Drought Computed: months with precip < 60mm Number of dry months (precipitation below 60mm threshold) in the block period. More dry months = longer fire season. Count [0-72] Per-pixel (~5.5km)
18 wind_speed_ms 💨 Wind ERA5-Land (10m wind) Mean wind speed at 10m. Wind fans flames and carries embers, increasing fire spread rate. m/s Per-pixel (~11km)
19 wind_direction_deg 💨 Wind ERA5-Land Mean wind direction. Determines fire spread direction relative to terrain slope and vegetation corridors. Degrees [0-360] Per-pixel (~11km)
20 FWI_proxy 🔥 Fire Weather Computed: temp_max_C × VPD_kPa / (precip_mean_mm + 1) Fire Weather Index proxy. Combines heat, atmospheric dryness, and rainfall into a single fire danger score. FINAL MODEL Composite index Per-pixel
21 elevation ⛰️ Terrain SRTM DEM (30m, resampled) Elevation above sea level. Higher elevations in Western Ghats have different forest types and fire regimes. FINAL MODEL meters Per-pixel (30m)
22 slope_deg ⛰️ Terrain SRTM DEM (derived) Terrain slope in degrees. Steeper slopes = faster uphill fire spread due to pre-heating of upslope fuel. Degrees Per-pixel (30m)
23 aspect_deg ⛰️ Terrain SRTM DEM (derived) Slope facing direction. South/SW-facing slopes in India receive more solar radiation = drier fuel. FINAL MODEL Degrees [0-360] Per-pixel (30m)
24 TWI ⛰️ Terrain SRTM DEM (derived: ln(a / tan β)) Topographic Wetness Index. Low TWI = dry ridges (fire-prone). High TWI = wet valleys (fire-resistant). Index Per-pixel (30m)
25 population_density 👥 Socio-Economic GHSL-POP / WorldPop (1km) Human population density. More people = more accidental ignition (slash-and-burn, campfires, arson). FINAL MODEL persons/km² Per-pixel (1km)

27 total_fire_count 🚫 Fire (excluded) MODIS FIRMS active fire Total number of fire detections at this pixel across the block period. Excluded from features (leakage). Count Per-pixel (1km)
28 FRP_mean_MW 🚫 Fire (excluded) MODIS FIRMS Mean Fire Radiative Power — intensity of fire in megawatts. Higher FRP = more intense blaze. Excluded (leakage). Megawatts Per-pixel (1km)
29 FRP_max_MW 🚫 Fire (excluded) MODIS FIRMS Maximum FRP — peak fire intensity at this pixel. Excluded (leakage). Megawatts Per-pixel (1km)
30 burned_area_binary 🚫 Fire (excluded) MODIS MCD64A1 Burned Area Binary: was this pixel mapped as burned? Excluded (leakage). Binary [0, 1] Per-pixel (500m)

🛰️ Google Earth Engine Data Sources

Every parameter comes from a specific satellite sensor or derived dataset, accessed through GEE's petabyte-scale catalog. Here are the 7 primary data sources:

🛰️ MODIS (Terra/Aqua) 17 params

Satellite: NASA's Terra (1999–) & Aqua (2002–) satellites
Resolution: 250m–1km, daily revisit
Products used:
• MOD13A2 — NDVI, EVI (500m, 16-day composites)
• MOD15A2H — LAI, FPAR (500m, 8-day)
• MCD12Q1 — Land Cover (500m, annual)
• MCD64A1 — Burned Area (500m, monthly)
Covers: Vegetation indices, leaf area, land type, burned area mapping

🔥 MODIS FIRMS 5 params

System: Fire Information for Resource Management System
Resolution: 1km, 4× daily
Detection: Thermal anomaly detection in MODIS bands 21/22 & 31
Products:
• Active fire detections (FRP, brightness temperature)
• Fire confidence level
• Fire occurrence binary flag
Covers: fire_occurrence (TARGET), FRP_mean/max, FIRMS_brightness, FIRMS_confidence, total_fire_count

⛰️ SRTM DEM 4 params

Mission: Shuttle Radar Topography Mission (2000)
Resolution: 30m (1 arc-second)
Coverage: 60°N to 56°S
Products:
• elevation — raw DEM value
• slope_deg — computed via ee.Terrain.slope()
• aspect_deg — computed via ee.Terrain.aspect()
• TWI — Topographic Wetness Index = ln(a/tan β)
Note: Static — same value across all 4 blocks

🌧️ CHIRPS 3 params

Dataset: Climate Hazards Group InfraRed Precipitation with Station data
Resolution: 0.05° (~5.5km), daily
Period: 1981–present
Products:
• precip_total_mm — sum over block period
• precip_mean_mm — daily average
• dry_months_count — months with <60mm
Key: Western Ghats gets 2000–5000mm/yr, but fire season (Jan–May) is very dry

🌍 ERA5-Land Reanalysis 6 params

Dataset: ECMWF ERA5-Land global reanalysis
Resolution: 0.1° (~11km), hourly
Period: 1950–present
Products:
• temp_mean/max/min_C — 2m air temperature
• relative_humidity_pct — 2m relative humidity
• VPD_kPa — Vapor Pressure Deficit
• wind_speed_ms, wind_direction_deg — 10m wind
Note: Reanalysis = satellite + ground stations + physics models combined

👥 GHSL / WorldPop 1 param

Dataset: Global Human Settlement Layer / WorldPop
Resolution: 1km
Product:
• population_density — persons/km²
Why: 90%+ of Indian forest fires are human-caused (slash-and-burn agriculture, NTFP collection, accidental)

📂 Parameter Categories Breakdown

The 36 raw parameters fall into 8 functional categories. Each category captures a different aspect of fire vulnerability:

🌿 Vegetation (8 params)

What: Greenness, canopy density, land type
Why for fire: Vegetation = fuel. Dry, deciduous forests with low NDVI_min are tinderboxes.
Params: NDVI_mean/max/min/seasonality, EVI_mean, LAI_mean, FPAR_mean, land_cover

🌡️ Temperature (3 params)

What: Air temperature statistics
Why for fire: Hot temps accelerate fuel drying and bring fuel closer to ignition point.
Params: temp_mean_C, temp_max_C, temp_min_C

🌧️ Precipitation & Moisture (5 params)

What: Rainfall, humidity, atmospheric dryness, drought duration
Why for fire: Drought is the #1 fire precondition. Low precip + high VPD = extreme fire danger.
Params: precip_total/mean_mm, relative_humidity_pct, VPD_kPa, dry_months_count

💨 Wind (2 params)

What: Wind speed and direction
Why for fire: Wind fans flames, carries embers (spotting), and determines fire spread direction.
Params: wind_speed_ms, wind_direction_deg

⛰️ Terrain (4 params)

What: Topography from SRTM radar
Why for fire: Fire moves faster uphill. South-facing slopes are drier. Ridges (low TWI) dry out first.
Params: elevation, slope_deg, aspect_deg, TWI

👥 Socio-Economic (1 param)

What: Human presence
Why for fire: Most Indian forest fires are anthropogenic — more people = more ignition sources.
Params: population_density

🔥 Fire Parameters (8 params)

What: Active fire detections and burned area
Role: 1 is the TARGET (fire_occurrence). The other 7 are EXCLUDED from features (would be leakage — you can't use current fire data to predict current fire).
Params: fire_occurrence⭐, total_fire_count, FRP_mean/max_MW, burned_area_binary, burn_month_count, FIRMS_brightness_K, FIRMS_confidence

✖️ GEE Pre-Engineered (5 params)

What: Composite/interaction features computed in GEE
Why: Capture non-linear relationships between variables.
Params: LFMC_proxy, FWI_proxy, climate_x_fuel, dryness_x_fuel, terrain_x_veg

⚙️ Full Data Pipeline — Parameter Journey

Here's exactly how parameters flow from raw satellite data to model input:

Step 1 — GEE Extraction
42 columns per CSV (4 CSVs × ~10K rows)
Google Earth Engine script extracts pixel values from 7 satellite products at each sample location. Exports 4 CSVs: WG_AGFE_Block1_2003_2008.csv through Block4_2021_2025.csv. Each pixel has lat/lon coordinates + 36 parameters + 6 metadata columns = 42 total columns.
Step 2 — Load & Merge
39,249 unified samples
4 block CSVs are concatenated into one DataFrame with a block_id column [1–4]. Fire rate: Block 1–3 ≈ 50%, Block 4 = 46%.
Step 3 — Spatial Thinning
39,249 → 29,216 samples (−25.6%)
KD-Tree removes points within 1km of each other to eliminate spatial autocorrelation. Without this, nearby pixels would leak information during cross-validation.
Step 4 — Column Separation
36 parameters → 28 environmental + 8 fire (excluded)
Remove 6 metadata columns. Separate 8 fire parameters (1 = target, 7 = excluded to prevent leakage). Remaining 28 environmental features proceed to VIF screening.
Step 5 — VIF Screening
28 → 14 (correlation) → 8 (VIF) environmental features
Pass 1: Remove 14 features with |ρ| > 0.75 (keeping the one with higher target correlation).
Pass 2: Remove 6 more with VIF > 10 iteratively.
Result: 8 independent environmental features.
Step 6 — Fire History Engineering
+10 prior-block fire features
For each block k ≥ 2, extract 7 lagged fire parameters from block k-1 at matching pixel locations. Add 3 derived features: fire_recurrence, fire_trend_delta, neighbor_fire_5km. Key finding: 63.1% of Block 4 fire pixels had fire in at least one prior block.
Step 7 — Temporal Deltas
+8 change-over-time features
For each of the 8 surviving environmental features, compute value(block k) − value(block k-1). Critical finding: Block 4 has delta_precip_total_mm = −721mm (severe drying trend!).
Step 8 — Climate Indices & Interactions
+2 climate indices + 5 interactions + 1 seasonal = +8
ONI/IOD from NOAA (block averages). 5 domain-knowledge interaction terms (e.g., FWI × NDVI, neighbor_fire × FWI). Plus fire_season = 1 constant.
Step 9 — Final Assembly
34 features × 29,216 samples → Train/Test Split
Train: Blocks 1–3 (22,321 samples) → SMOTE → 27,706 balanced samples
Test: Block 4 (6,895 samples) — completely unseen future data
StandardScaler applied to all 34 features. Checkpointed to disk.
Step 10 — 5 ML Models
RF (93.7%) | XGBoost (93.1%) | LightGBM (93.3%) | CatBoost (79.4%) | MLP (80.0%)
Each model trained with Optuna hyperparameter tuning (15–25 trials). All models receive the same 34-feature input. Best overall: Random Forest (93.68% accuracy, 0.9739 AUC).
Key Insight: The 36 raw parameters are NOT area-based or index-based in the traditional sense — they are pixel-level satellite measurements at each geographic coordinate. The "index" is purely spatial: each row = one 500m × 500m pixel in the Western Ghats, identified by its latitude + longitude. The temporal dimension comes from the 4 block periods (2003–2025), not from any time-series index.
