▎Phase A — Study Design & Data Collection
1

🗺️ Study Area Definition

Focus on Western Ghats — UNESCO World Heritage biodiversity hotspot spanning 1,600 km along India's west coast. Boundary defined using RESOLVE Ecoregions 2017 (~160,000 km²). Three sub-regions: Northern WG · Central WG · Southern WG.

RESOLVE Ecoregions 6 States 8°N – 21.9°N
2

📅 Temporal Block Design

22 years split into 4 temporal blocks — captures 4–5 complete ENSO cycles. Blocks 1–3 → Training (80%), Block 4 → Testing (20%). No random split — strict chronological order prevents future data leakage.

🔵 Block 1 · 2003–2008 · 10K
🔵 Block 2 · 2009–2014 · 10K
🔵 Block 3 · 2015–2020 · 10K
🔴 Block 4 · 2021–2025 · 9.2K

▎Phase B — Preprocessing & Feature Engineering
4

🧹 Data Preprocessing Pipeline

Five sequential cleaning steps ensure model-ready features with no data leakage.

① Spatial Thinning · 1 km min distance
② Non-Fire Sampling · 1:3 fire ratio
③ VIF Screening · remove |ρ|>0.75, VIF<10
④ SMOTE · balance train set only
⑤ StandardScaler · fit on train only

▎Phase C — AHP Weighting & Stacking Ensemble
6

⚖️ AHP Driver Group Weighting

Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) assigns learned softmax weights to 4 driver groups. Each sub-zone gets its own weights — optimized via Bayesian search (Optuna, 50 trials per zone).

🏗️ Stacking Ensemble — 5 Base + Meta-Learner

Five diverse base learners generate out-of-fold predictions via 5-fold cross-validation. A Ridge meta-learner optimally combines them. Two-layer architecture consistently outperforms any single model.

▎Phase D — Uncertainty, Explainability & Evaluation
8

🛡️ Conformal Prediction — Uncertainty Quantification

Distribution-free uncertainty method providing guaranteed coverage. For every prediction, outputs a set {0}, {1}, or {0,1} with ≥90% coverage guarantee. Three uncertainty sources: conformal sets · MC dropout · ensemble disagreement.

Distribution-Free Coverage ≥ 90% Novel Contribution
9

🔍 SHAP Explainability — Why Did It Predict Fire?

TreeSHAP provides feature attribution at 4 levels: group-level (weather vs fuel vs topo vs human), feature-level (which variable?), temporal-level (which lag?), and zone-level (how drivers differ across sub-regions).
